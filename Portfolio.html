§ 01 — About the designer

The seam between rigour and romance.

I am a designer shaped by two disciplines. By training a software engineer, by vocation a fashion designer — I lead a team of over 100 people and, every evening, I return to the pattern table. Rigour from one world; romance from the other.

My work lives in the seam between the technical and the feminine. Embroidery, ruffles, gathered volumes, hand-finished detail — all built on patterns drafted with the same precision I bring to a product roadmap.

“A garment is a blueprint that happens to breathe.”

I am applying to Inditex because I want to marry craft at scale with the poetics of how a woman feels when she gets dressed.

§ 02 — Design philosophy

Three claves
of the house.

The codes I return to, collection after collection — drawn from the moodboard of my own hand.

01

Artisanal detail — embroidery, jaretas, hand-finishing.

Every garment carries at least one gesture that could only be made by a human hand. Seed-stitch florals, drawn-thread work, tiny pintucks that catch the light differently than a machine ever could.

02

Femininity through fabric & ribbon.

Softness is a material choice first. Cotton poplin with body, organza with memory, velvet ribbon tied — never sewn flat — so it can be undone, retied, lived in.

03

Volume through layers, ruffles & gathers.

Silhouettes built by addition: tiers, peplum, fichu collars, gathered sleeves set high on the shoulder. The body stays still; the garment moves for her.

01 · SketchPlate 01a

First pass — silhouette & attitude.

drop sketch

Twin figure study. I draft the attitude before the garment — how she stands, where the weight sits, which hand she keeps in the pocket. The collar and ruffled shoulder line are decided here, before any seam is drawn.

02 · PatternPlate 01b

Flat — block draft & seam allowances.

drop pattern

Block drafted on the flat — 1.5 cm seam allowance, 4 cm hem. The fichu collar is cut separately and rolled onto the body; this is the one seam that decides whether the whole thing reads romantic or theatrical.

Standing collar with two-finger ease. Sleeve cap raised 1 cm above the shoulder point — this is what gives the blouse its quiet authority without volume tipping into costume.

02 · PatternPlate 02b

Blouse block & embroidery placement.

drop pattern

Embroidery zone marked before cutting — I lay the floral motif on the flat pattern first, so the hoop sits square on-grain. The sleeve cap carries five gathering notches at 1.5× ratio.

drop photo

Satin stitch petals, French knot centres. Forty-two hours at the frame. The stitches don't want to be perfect — they want to be visibly made by a hand that knew where it was going.
